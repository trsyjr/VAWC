"It Was Never About the Clothes"

Date: 1967

Victim Name

Maggie de la Riva

Case Summary

In 1967, actress Maggie de la Riva was abducted at gunpoint and raped by four young men from wealthy and influential families. The perpetrators—Jaime José, Basilio Pineda, Rogelio Cañal, and Edgardo Aquino—believed their status would protect them from accountability. De la Riva was subjected to intense trauma and humiliation, and the case became highly publicized. Despite widespread victim-blaming attitudes during that era, she refused to be silenced. She bravely identified her attackers, rejected bribes and intimidation, and chose to testify in open court—a rare and courageous move for women at the time.

De la Riva’s resolve played a decisive role in securing justice. The accused were convicted, and in 1972, three of them were executed by electric chair, marking one of the most notable rape convictions in Philippine criminal history. The case highlighted the systemic bias favoring influential suspects and shed light on the strength required for survivors to pursue justice. Ultimately, Maggie de la Riva’s courage transformed her personal ordeal into a landmark case that challenged stigma and affirmed that no social status can place criminals above the law.

Details

Actress Maggie de la Riva was abducted and raped by four influential men in 1967. Despite attempts to silence her through intimidation and bribery, she pursued justice and testified publicly, a courageous act during a time when rape was heavily stigmatized. Her resilience led to the conviction of the perpetrators.

Date: 2019

Victim Name

Christine Lee Silawan

Case Summary

"In March 2019, Christine Lee Silawan, a 16-year-old student from Lapu-Lapu City, Cebu, was found brutally murdered in a vacant lot after being lured out through social media. Investigators reported that she had been deceived by someone posing as another person online, a tactic known as catfishing. Her body showed signs of severe violence, including indications of sexual assault, shocking both the local community and the entire nation. The brutality of the crime, combined with the fact that she was a young minor, intensified public outcry and calls for justice.

The investigation later revealed that a teenage suspect—her former boyfriend—was allegedly behind the crime, with police tracing his involvement through digital footprints and chat messages. Christine’s death sparked nationwide conversations about the dangers of online manipulation, the vulnerability of young women to predatory behavior on social media, and the urgent need for stronger protections for minors in digital spaces. Above all, her case served as a painful reminder of how online interactions can escalate into real-world harm, emphasizing the importance of digital safety and early intervention to protect young people."

Details

Christine, a 16-year-old student from Lapu-Lapu City, was found murdered in a vacant lot after being lured out through social media. Investigators confirmed she had been sexually assaulted. Her case shook the nation and highlighted the vulnerability of young women to predatory behavior online.

Date: 1991

Victim Name

Carmela Vizconde

Case Summary

The Vizconde murders occurred in 1991 in Parañaque, Philippines, when seventeen-year-old Carmela Vizconde, along with her mother, Estrellita, and younger sister, Jennifer, were brutally attacked inside their home. Carmela was raped and murdered, while her mother and sister were also killed in the same violent incident. The crime shocked the nation due to its extreme brutality and the apparent targeting of an entire family. The case quickly drew massive media attention and became emblematic of violence against women and the broader issue of family-targeted crimes.

The legal proceedings following the murders were highly controversial and spanned over a decade, highlighting weaknesses in the judicial process, including issues of evidence handling, witness credibility, and procedural delays. Initially, several accused individuals were convicted, but subsequent appeals and retrials brought further confusion and public debate. The Vizconde case remains a pivotal reference in discussions on criminal justice reform, victim protection, and the societal response to violence against women in the Philippines, underscoring the urgent need for an efficient and transparent legal system."

Details

Seventeen-year-old Carmela Vizconde was raped and murdered along with her mother and sister inside their home in Parañaque. The brutality of the crime and the long, controversial legal saga exposed weaknesses in the judicial system. Carmela’s case remains one of the most discussed VAW cases in Philippine history.

Date: 2014

Victim Name

Jennifer Laude Case

Case Summary

The Jennifer Laude case occurred in 2014 in Olongapo City, Philippines, when Jennifer Laude, a transgender Filipina, was brutally killed inside a motel by a U.S. Marine. The perpetrator reportedly attacked her upon discovering her gender identity, leading to her death. This heinous act brought national and international attention to the dangers faced by transgender individuals and highlighted the pervasive issue of transphobic violence in society. Jennifer’s death became a rallying point for advocacy on LGBTQ+ rights, justice for hate crimes, and the broader struggle against discrimination and violence targeting marginalized communities.

The legal proceedings surrounding the case were closely followed, with debates on jurisdiction, accountability of foreign military personnel, and the adequacy of protective measures for vulnerable populations. Jennifer Laude’s story underscored the urgent need for societal recognition of LGBTQ+ rights and protection against hate-motivated crimes, prompting calls for justice, equality, and systemic reforms to safeguard the lives and dignity of transgender and other marginalized individuals."

Details

Jennifer Laude, a transgender Filipina, was killed inside a motel in Olongapo by a U.S. Marine who violently attacked her upon learning her identity. Her death spotlighted transphobic violence and the vulnerability of LGBTQ+ individuals to hate-motivated crimes.

Date: 1993

Victim Name

Eileen Sarmenta & Allan Gomez

Case Summary

In 1993, University of the Philippines student Eileen Sarmenta and her friend Allan Gomez were abducted, raped, and murdered by a group led by then-Calauan Mayor Antonio Sanchez and his cohorts. The crime was premeditated and marked by extreme brutality, with court documents revealing a chilling abuse of power and disregard for human life. Eileen Sarmenta’s abduction and assault, followed by the murder of both victims, shocked the nation and highlighted the dangers posed by influential individuals exploiting their authority to commit heinous acts.

The case became a landmark in the Philippines for its focus on accountability and the fight against impunity. After lengthy trials, Antonio Sanchez and his accomplices were convicted, sending a strong message about the consequences of abusing power. The story of Eileen Sarmenta and Allan Gomez remains a powerful symbol in advocating for women’s rights, protection of the vulnerable, and raising awareness against violence perpetrated by those in positions of authority."

Details

University student Eileen Sarmenta was abducted, raped, and murdered by the group of then-Mayor Antonio Sanchez. Court documents detailed the premeditated nature of the crime. Her story became a symbol of the fight against abuse of power.

Date: 2017

Incident

Bulacan Massacre

Case Summary

"The Bulacan Massacre occurred in 2017 when a family in Bulacan became the target of a brutal home invasion. During the attack, a mother and her daughter were sexually assaulted and murdered, along with several other family members. The extreme violence in what was considered a safe family environment sent shockwaves across the nation and highlighted the grave vulnerability of women and children even within the home. The case drew attention to the pervasive risks of gender-based violence and the urgent need for stronger protective measures for families.

The incident exposed gaps in community safety and law enforcement response, prompting discussions on preventive strategies against such violent crimes. It remains a chilling reminder that violence against women can happen anywhere and underscores the importance of justice, accountability, and public awareness to protect the most vulnerable members of society."

Details

In a gruesome home invasion in Bulacan, a mother and daughter were sexually assaulted and killed along with several family members. The case shocked the country and exposed how gender-based violence can occur even in supposedly safe environments like the family home.
